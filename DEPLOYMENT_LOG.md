## 2026-07-11 — Real-court-geometry zone mapping (Phase G): homography+fallback, BWF service-line row bands, white-line corner refinement
- **Changes**: Implements `docs/PRD_v2.6.md` Phase G. All opt-in via the existing (now wired-up) `--homography` CLI flag — default pipeline behavior (no flag) is unchanged.
  - `src/main.py`: added `--homography` flag → `AnalysisPipeline(use_homography=...)` → `calibrate_from_video(..., use_homography=...)` (previously hardcoded `False` with no way to enable it from the CLI at all).
  - `src/pipeline/zone_grid.py`: added `zone_number_real()` / `_row_band_real()` — row banding at the real BWF short/long service line fractions (`FRONT_BAND_FRAC≈0.7045`, `BACK_BAND_FRAC≈0.1134`) instead of equal thirds. `zone_number()` (equal-thirds) is unchanged and still used by the proportional pixel-space fallback — this is a deliberate scope split, not an oversight (see module docstring).
  - `src/pipeline/court_detector.py`: `court_coords_to_zone()` now calls `zone_number_real()` instead of `zone_number()`, since its inputs are true court-plane fractions. Added `refine_corners_with_lines()` — snaps the green-surface-derived coarse corners to detected white boundary lines (angle+proximity matched Hough segments, `cv2.fitLine`, re-intersected per corner), with a fallback-safe design (reverts to the coarse corner, per-edge or entirely, on low confidence or an implausibly large snap). Wired into `calibrate_homography()` immediately after `detect_court_corners()`.
  - `src/pipeline/debug_overlay.py`: `_draw_court_grid()` now draws the real perspective-correct homography trapezoid (`_draw_homography_grid`, new) when a homography is active, instead of always drawing the flat proportional rectangle regardless — the row lines use the same BWF fractions as `zone_number_real`, kept in lockstep on purpose.
  - `src/pipeline/court_calibration.py`: `recalibrate_from_shuttle_positions()` **bug fix** — was unconditionally clearing `homography` on its output; with `--homography` enabled this would have silently reverted every shot's zone to the proportional grid right after this second calibration pass ran, before any shot was ever actually scored through the homography. Now preserves homography; only `top`/`bottom`/`net_y` (the fallback's own bounds) are refined by this pass.
- **Tests**: `tests/test_zone_grid.py` (+9: BWF fraction correctness/asymmetry, `_row_band_real` thresholds/clamping, `zone_number_real` agreement with `zone_for_bands` across all bands/halves, a concrete equal-thirds-vs-real-bands divergence case). `tests/test_court_detector.py` (new, 10 tests: `court_coords_to_zone` at the real service-line boundaries for both halves; `refine_corners_with_lines` on a synthetic white-rectangle-on-green frame — snaps a 12px-shifted coarse estimate to <3px error, falls back unchanged on a blank frame with no lines, and falls back unchanged on an implausibly-wrong coarse estimate rather than "correcting" it toward an unverified answer). `tests/test_court_calibration.py` (1 test flipped: `test_preserves_homography_on_the_recalibrated_result`, formerly asserted homography was cleared — that was the bug). `pytest` — 66/66 pass (13 new/changed). `ruff check` clean on every new/touched file (remaining repo-wide findings are pre-existing, in dead-code files this change doesn't touch).
- **Manual verification**: Re-ran the full pipeline end-to-end on `Badminton_video_example.mp4` with `--homography --debug-frames 10 --debug-video` (real YOLO + cached TrackNetV3 predictions, no mocks) both before and after this change, to confirm the fixes visually: (1) zoomed into the same corner region of the same sampled frame before/after `refine_corners_with_lines` — the drawn grid boundary moved from visibly inside the true green/blue mat edge to hugging it; (2) confirmed the drawn zone labels now show a large mid (4/5/6) band and thin front/back bands, matching the BWF line positions instead of equal thirds. Quantitative re-validation against video 1's ground truth CSV (comparing the shipped proportional grid vs. homography-only vs. the homography+fallback hybrid, on the same real shuttle positions from the actual pipeline run) is documented in `docs/reports/court_zone_homography_vs_proportional.html`: homography roughly halves row/col distributional divergence vs. the shipped method, at the cost of coverage (85%/50.6% across the two videos, homography-only) — which is why the hybrid (100% coverage on both), not homography-only, is what `--homography` actually enables.
- **Follow-ups** (also tracked in `docs/PRD_v2.6.md` Open Questions): (1) `--homography` remains opt-in, not the default — a combined end-to-end re-validation against ground truth with BWF banding + corner refinement both layered on top of the G.1 hybrid (rather than G.1 alone) is the concrete next step before proposing that. (2) `refine_corners_with_lines()` is only validated on synthetic data so far; a real-video corner-accuracy re-measurement (same visual-overlay + coverage methodology as G.1) is the next validation step, not yet done. (3) Whether corner-detection robustness eventually needs a trained keypoint model instead of classical CV (Phase G.4) is left open, gated on `calibrate_homography`'s existing `num_valid_samples` signal across more videos than the two currently available.

## 2026-07-03 — Fix back-row/mid-row zone collapse via shuttle-derived row recalibration
- **Changes**: `_detect_shots()` in `src/main.py` now runs a second calibration pass after shot detection. Root cause (found via a visual audit overlaying the calibrated grid, YOLO boxes, and TrackNetV3 shuttle points on real frames): the row-axis bounds (`top`/`bottom`/`net_y`) were derived from player-FOOT positions, which don't reach as far toward the baseline as the shuttle does — on video 1 this placed the back-row/mid-row boundary only ~3px from the real baseline, so nearly the entire "back row" zone band fell off the actual court surface, and any real back-court shot got bucketed as "mid." Added `recalibrate_from_shuttle_positions()` (`src/pipeline/court_calibration.py`) which re-derives `top`/`bottom`/`net_y` from the 5th/95th percentile of *real* TrackNetV3 shuttle landing Y-values (not the lunge-apex proxy) once shots are known, leaving `left`/`right` (column axis) untouched since that axis was already well-fit. Parameters (percentile pair, outward margin, blend-with-original alpha) were chosen via a sweep against video 1's ground truth. A second variant explored during investigation (hybrid: homography-derived row + proportional column) was tested, found to underperform the chosen approach, and removed rather than left half-wired.
- **Tests**: Added `tests/test_court_calibration.py` (6 new unit tests covering `recalibrate_from_shuttle_positions`: too-few-samples fallback, percentile/margin formula correctness, left/right and metadata pass-through, homography clearing, alpha=0 no-op, and exclusion of malformed `receive_by` values). `pytest` — 6/6 pass. `ruff check` clean on all touched files (3 pre-existing, unrelated lint issues remain elsewhere in `main.py` — unused import, an f-string without placeholders, one over-length line — not touched, to keep this change scoped).
- **Manual verification**: Ran the full pipeline end-to-end on both test videos (`python -m src.main`, real YOLO + TrackNetV3, no mocks).
  - Video 1 vs ground truth (70 shots/6 rallies, n=53 matched by rally+position): zone exact-match 13.2%→**17.0%**, zone exact-or-adjacent 60.4%→**64.2%**, row-distribution divergence from ground truth 0.528→**0.340** (back/mid/front moved from 17.0/47.2/35.8 to 18.9/37.7/43.4 vs true 34.0/20.8/45.3), column-axis divergence unchanged at 0.038 (confirms the fix is row-only, as designed).
  - Video 2 (no ground truth, plausibility check): front-row share went from 2.4% (baseline — front zones were nearly erased) to **37.8%**, now spread across 8 of 9 zones instead of collapsing into 2-3.
- **Follow-ups**: Not a complete fix — zone-exact-or-adjacent still well under the PRD's 80% target, and the tuning sweep found configs with even higher adjacent-accuracy (67.9%) that were rejected because they cost row-fit; further iteration on the row-axis signal (rather than more hybrid combinations with homography, which was tried and didn't help) is the likely next lever. Separately, the same investigation surfaced an unrelated bug worth a follow-up: rally 1's current 6-second intro-skip is too short — a static tournament-title graphic at t=9.8s was processed as live rally footage.
